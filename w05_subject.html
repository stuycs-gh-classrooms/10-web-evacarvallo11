Rhythms

In order to read music, we must first understand the duration of different kinds of notes. A whole note has a duration of 4 beats.

A half note has a duration of 2 beats. 2 half notes are equal to one whole note.

A quarter note has a duration of 1 beat. 4 quarter notes are equal to one whole note.

An eighth note has a duration of 1/2 of a beat. 8 eighth notes are equal to one whole note. When reading music with eighth notes, we can subdivide the beats in half. Instead of counting 1-2-3-4, we can count 1-and 2-and 3-and 4-and, whereas each "and" represents an eighth note.

Lastly, a sixteenth note has a duration of 1/4 of a beat. Therefore, 4 eighth notes are equal to one beat. When reading music with sixteenth notes, we can subdivide the beats in fourths. Instead of counting 1-2-3-4, we can count 1-e-and-a 2-e-and-a 3-e-and-a 4-e-and-a, whereas each "e," "and," and "a" represents a sixteenth note.
