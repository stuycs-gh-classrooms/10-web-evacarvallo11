Steps and Accidentals

In music, we measure the space between notes based on half and whole steps. A half step is the distance between one key on a piano to the adjacent key. A whole step is the same distance as two half steps. An accidental is a symbol used to raise or lower the pitch of a note. A flat lowers the pitch of a note by a half step. A sharp raises the pitch of a note by a half step.

While most of the notes we already learned are a whole step away from each other on the piano, the distance between E-F and B-C is only a half step. This means that E sharp is the same as F, and B sharp is the same as C. In addition, since we can sharp and flat all notes, there are other equivalent notes as well. For example, G sharp is the same as A flat.
